How to Tell If Your Contractor Estimate Is Fair (Without Being an Expert)

You got a contractor bid for your kitchen renovation. It says 7,000. Is that fair? Too high? A steal? Unless you have done this exact project before in your exact market, you have no idea. And the contractor knows that.

The Information Asymmetry Problem

Contractors know material costs, labor rates, and markup percentages. Homeowners do not. This information gap means homeowners overpay on an estimated 30-40% of contractor jobs, according to consumer advocacy groups.

Getting three bids helps, but if all three contractors are overpriced in the same way, three bids just gives you three overpriced options.

What a Fair Estimate Looks Like

A fair contractor estimate should break down into:

  • Materials -- Verifiable against supplier prices (Home Depot, local distributors)
  • Labor -- Should match local prevailing wage rates for the trade
  • Overhead -- Typically 10-20% for insurance, truck, tools, office
  • Profit margin -- Industry standard is 10-20% on top of costs

If a bid does not break these out, that is a red flag.
